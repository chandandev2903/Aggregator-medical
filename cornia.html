Conditions We Treat

Types of Cornea Conditions

Conditions affecting the cornea can vary greatly in severity. Early diagnosis is key to effective treatment.

🦠
Most Common

Infections

Bacterial, viral or fungal infections can lead to painful corneal ulcers that require prompt medical attention. Keratitis is the most prevalent form and responds well to early treatment.

🔬

Degenerative Conditions

Keratoconus or Fuchs' dystrophy slowly damages the cornea over time and diminishes vision progressively.

⚡

Trauma

Physical damage from accidents or chemical burns can cause corneal scarring, sometimes permanently affecting vision.

🧮

Genetic Disorders

Congenital dystrophies and other hereditary conditions can impact vision quality early in life.

🌿

Allergies

Severe allergies can break down the ocular surface and cause chronic irritation and discomfort.

Advanced Technology

Cornea Diagnosis

At Shraddha Eye Care Trust, we use advanced diagnostic tools to assess the health of the cornea with pinpoint accuracy.

🔭

Anterior Segment OCT

Non-invasive, high-resolution imaging that provides a cut-section of the cornea and analyses the anterior segment with exceptional detail.

💡

Slit-Lamp Examination

A bright, focused light magnifies the eye, allowing our specialists to closely examine the cornea and detect subtle abnormalities.

📏

Pachymetry

Measures the thickness of the cornea, which is important for assessing glaucoma risk, LASIK suitability, or the need for corneal transplant surgery.

📐

Keratometry

Assesses the curvature of the cornea to detect irregularities like keratoconus and plan appropriate treatment approaches.

Risks & Complications of Cornea Transplant

While a cornea transplant can significantly improve vision, it's important to understand the possible risks involved.

🔴

Post-Surgery Swelling or Infection

Temporary swelling or risk of infection in the immediate post-operative period, managed with medications.

⚠️

Donor Cornea Rejection

In rare cases, the body may reject the donor cornea. Early detection allows for effective management.

🌫️

Blurry Vision During Recovery

Blurry vision in the early stages of recovery, which usually improves significantly over time.

🛡️

Graft Failure (Rare)

Though rare, graft failure can occur and can be managed effectively with early intervention by specialists.

These risks are minimized through proper aftercare, medications, and regular follow-ups with your doctor at Shraddha Eye Care Trust.
Stay Protected

Prevention of Corneal Diseases

Protecting your cornea is simple and effective. Small daily habits can prevent serious corneal conditions from developing.

🕶️

Wear Sunglasses

Block harmful UV rays with quality sunglasses when outdoors.

📱

Limit Screen Time

Reduce dry eye by taking regular breaks from digital screens.

🥕

Balanced Diet

Eat foods rich in Vitamin A such as leafy greens and carrots.

👁️

Good Eye Hygiene

Maintain eye hygiene, especially for contact lens users.

⛑️

Protective Eyewear

Use protective eyewear during activities like sports or construction work to prevent corneal injuries and trauma.
